A. IFVG INVERSION TRIGGER
   What EXACTLY constitutes a valid IFVG inversion?
========================================================================

RULING: CANDLE BODY CLOSE beyond the FVG far edge. NOT wicks.

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ (full_study.txt, primary educator):
   "I don't want a weak close. I don't want to close with a huge wick at
    the top. I want a body close so far above this gap. It literally just
    disrespects the gap."

   "We get like a 20 point close above."

   "Did we close above by a tick or did we close above by a few points?
    Like is it just a very tiny tiny close or is it obvious? Is it noticeable?"

2. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Close/Entry section):
   "Is the candle mainly a body or mainly a wick"
   "The close above/below the FVG"
   "Avoid closes that are only a few ticks through the FVG"
   "BODIES TELL STORY - WICKS DO DAMAGE"

3. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Stop Hunt section):
   "A Stop Hunt/Sweep that prints a wick at the high or low
    (no candle body closure above)"
   -- This confirms wick = sweep/damage, body close = directional signal

4. BionicNQ bad example:
   "Barely closes through FVG" + "Huge reversal wick" = FAIL
   vs. "2 candle close - big displacement" + "Mainly body" = PASS

5. Online consensus (TrinityTrading, Tradezella):
   "A simple touch or wick through the gap does not create an IFVG.
    Price must close fully beyond the gap boundaries."
   "Entry on candle closure through IFVG if there is clear displacement"

ACTIONABLE RULE FOR CODE:
  - LONG (bearish FVG inversion): candle.body_high > fvg.top
    (i.e., candle CLOSE > fvg.top for a green candle)
  - SHORT (bullish FVG inversion): candle.body_low < fvg.bottom
    (i.e., candle CLOSE < fvg.bottom for a red candle)
  - The close must be MEANINGFULLY beyond, not just 1 tick:
    current bot uses >= 2pt, which aligns with sources
  - Body must dominate the candle (body >= 50% of total range):
    current bot checks this, which aligns with "mainly a body" rule
  - Wicks alone do NOT count as inversion

STATUS: Current bot implementation is CORRECT.

B. ENTRY TIMING
   Entry at close of inversion candle, or wait for pullback?
========================================================================

RULING: MARKET ORDER at the CLOSE of the inversion candle. No waiting.

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Close/Entry section):
   "Once closed, immediately market order your entry."
   "Setting a limit order, may get preferred RR, but will make you miss
    setups in the long run."

2. BionicNQ (full_study.txt):
   "Enter here" (pointing to inversion candle close in every example)
   "I had my stop loss below the gap and I targeted this 15 minute"
   -- Consistent pattern: entry is AT the close, not after retest

3. BionicNQ acknowledges a retest alternative exists but does NOT use it:
   "I could look to trade it in here on the open of the next higher time
    frame candle" -- but this is described as a separate, more conservative
    approach for when the inversion candle is bearish/unconvincing

4. Online (Tradezella):
   Two approaches documented:
   - Aggressive: "Market order when you see the IFVG form"
   - Conservative: "Limit order inside the IFVG zone, wait for retest"
   BionicNQ explicitly uses the aggressive approach.

5. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on when NOT to enter at close:
   "If we don't have a candle which supports expansion... we're then
    waiting for a candle two to form."
   -- Exception: when the inversion candle itself is unconvincing (bearish
      close when expecting bullish move), wait for next HTF candle open.

EXCEPTION SCENARIO (from transcripts):
   If the inversion candle is a COUNTER-DIRECTION candle (e.g., bearish
   candle body closing above a bearish FVG for a long), BionicNQ says:
   "I'm not expecting it to be an expansion candle. I'm just expecting it
    to go back towards its opening price and form a reversal candle."
   In this case, wait for the NEXT HTF candle open.

ACTIONABLE RULE FOR CODE:
  - Primary: entry = market order at inversion candle close price
  - No pullback/retest waiting in the primary model
  - The "wait for next candle" exception is too discretionary to code
    reliably; the body-dominance filter already eliminates most of these
    cases (a bearish candle closing above a gap would fail body >= 50%)

STATUS: Current bot implementation is CORRECT.

C. TIMEFRAME PRIORITY FOR IFVG
   Which TF's FVGs take priority? Must ALL of that TF invert?
========================================================================

RULING: HIGHEST TF first (5m > 4m > 3m > 2m > 1m). BionicNQ is very
clear that higher TF inversions carry more weight and lower TF inversions
alone are "bare minimum" setups.

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Bare Minimum PO3):
   "Only trade PO3 with 15m+ or 5m sponsored FVGs (BEST FIX)"
   "1m swing/5m FVG PDI model is notorious for this"
   -- 1m swing / 5m gap = bare minimum, not preferred

2. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (HTF Sponsorship):
   "HTF manipulation that distributes, creating a sponsored LTF LEG"
   "'Only 1 gap is sponsored'... The entire LEG is sponsored"
   -- The entire manipulation leg's FVGs matter, not just one

3. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Bare Minimum IFVG):
   "1m swing/5m FVG mech model is famous for this"
   "Only trade with A/A+ LTF structure"
   -- Again: 1m/5m is bare minimum; higher TF preferred

4. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on close speed relative to TF:
   "I'm not expecting like a five minute fair value gap to close like on
    the first candle. I usually let two to three candles relative to that
    time frame to close."
   "If it's a one minute fair value gap, I would give the inversion like
    3 minutes max four."
   -- Higher TF FVGs naturally take longer; this is expected

5. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on multiple FVGs:
   "There are multiple fair value gaps here. So, in this case, I want to
    use all of those fair value gaps and treat it like one big fair value
    gap. And you can see we do not go and just close through it."
   -- ALL FVGs must be cleared, not just one

6. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on 15m/30m context:
   "High time frame PDA, I'm really just going to be using 15 minute fair
    value gaps, 30 minute fair value gaps. Maybe an hourly."
   -- HTF FVGs (15m+) for key levels/bias; LTF (1-5m) for entry

ACTIONABLE RULE FOR CODE:
  - TF_PRIORITY = [5, 4, 3, 2, 1] (current implementation)
  - Find the HIGHEST TF that has FVGs on the manipulation leg
  - ALL FVGs of that highest TF on the leg must invert before entry fires
  - If no FVGs exist at highest TF, fall to next TF down
  - "Treat it like one big fair value gap" = all gaps of same TF on
    the leg must be closed through
  - Speed gate: inversion should complete within ~3x the TF in candle
    count (e.g., 5m FVG: allow ~3 five-minute candles = 15 1m candles)

STATUS: Current bot implementation (Priority 1+2) is CORRECT.
  The "all FVGs of highest TF must invert" rule is implemented.

D. BREAKEVEN WITH LIQUIDITY
   When to move SL to breakeven? Does intermediate liquidity trigger BE?
========================================================================

RULING: BE is CONDITIONAL and discretionary. Not automatic at 1R.

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Close/Entry section):
   "Go BE only when reversal probability is high (HTF FVG, SMT, ERL)
    and weigh conviction against LTF liq/imb for a decision on BE"
   "Closures that jeopardize RR or close BE can be avoided"
   "I usually take them if High Conviction and hold for 1:1 n pray for
    no BE"

2. BionicNQ (full_study.txt):
   "I got my one to one and I was out of there."
   -- In this case, 1:1 was the TP, not the BE trigger

3. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on conviction vs targets:
   "You could have taken a TP here, right? This could be low hanging fruit.
    Now, in this scenario, I actually didn't take low hanging fruit, but
    that's because my conviction was so high."
   -- High conviction = hold past intermediate levels
   -- Low conviction = take low-hanging fruit early

4. Tradezella (Josh's IFVG model):
   "If the first internal liquidity is hit and no further displacement
    occurs, assume we're rotating and protect capital."

5. Online general ICT:
   "Once price moves favorably by 1:1 risk-reward, consider moving your
    stop to breakeven."

ACTIONABLE RULE FOR CODE:
  For a mechanical bot (no discretion), implement as:

  - DO NOT auto-BE at 1R blindly
  - Instead: scan for OPPOSING liquidity levels between entry and TP
  - If an opposing major level exists between entry and TP:
    * If price reaches that level, TAKE PARTIAL or FULL TP there
    * Move SL to breakeven
  - If NO opposing liquidity between entry and TP:
    * Let the trade run to full TP
    * BE trigger at 1R as safety fallback
  - Opposing liquidity = PDH/PDL, session H/L, EQH/EQL, HTF FVG edge
    that could cause reversal

  Simplified mechanical version (recommended for bot):
  - be_trigger_r = 1.0 (current) as MINIMUM safety
  - If opposing ERL exists between entry and TP at < 1R distance:
    adjust TP to that level instead (already handled by DOL logic)
  - Priority 6 (BE at first internal H/L) from CLAUDE.md is
    LOWER priority and should be tested separately

STATUS: Current bot uses be_trigger_r=1.0 which is a reasonable
  mechanical approximation. The discretionary "reversal probability"
  assessment cannot be fully automated.

E. SWEEP QUALITY
   What makes a valid sweep vs a random spike/micro-tap?
========================================================================

RULING: A valid sweep requires (1) wick penetration beyond the level,
(2) body closes BACK INSIDE the range, (3) context of WHERE it happens
(HTF key level), and (4) fast reaction / displacement after.

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Stop Hunt):
   "A Stop Hunt/Sweep that prints a wick at the high or low
    (no candle body closure above)"
   "Shows most strength through a fast reaction -> distribution (IFVG)"
   "SL should offer 0 open liquidity and 0 unmitigated imbalances"
   "Stop Hunt into/inside HTF Key Level (FVG)"
   "Ideally sweeps an Accumulation"
   "Bonus Points if Sweep is after HTF candle opens (PO3)"

2. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (Mastering Manipulation):
   "HTF Key Level + Significant Liquidity Sweep + SMT (cherry on top)"
   "BODIES TELL STORY - WICKS DO DAMAGE"
   -- Sweep = wick damage (long wick, small body at the extreme)
   -- Distribution = body tells story (displacement candle after)

3. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Stop Hunt examples):
   PASS: "First entry - 0 Stop Hunt/Open Liq SL - Discount/Bottom of range"
         "Second Entry - HR SL - more engineered targets"
   FAIL: "No Stop hunt - Fomo/DoL focused"
   FAIL: "5m closes slow, leaves LR, and 0 stop hunt"

4. BionicNQ (full_study.txt):
   "Clean stop hunt, hit the hourly gap, super good inversion"
   "This is not just a random high, a random low. This can be session
    liquidity, previous day highs and lows or significant swings"

5. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on what the sweep MUST hit:
   "What what's like that criteria that you're looking for? Number one
    thing is going to be that high time frame key level. This will be like
    15-minute fair value gaps, 30 minute fair value gaps, 4 hour fair
    value gaps."

6. Online (general ICT):
   "The body of the candle must close back inside the previous trading
    range. If the body closes outside the range, it's a breakout, not
    a sweep."
   "Sweeps create large wicks, while displacement creates large bodies.
    Mostly wick = sweep. Mostly body = displacement."

ACTIONABLE RULES FOR CODE:

  Sweep candle requirements:
  1. Wick penetrates beyond the liquidity level price
  2. Body closes BACK INSIDE (body_low >= level for bearish sweep,
     body_high <= level for bullish sweep) -- ALREADY IMPLEMENTED
  3. Body dominance: body >= 50% of candle range -- ALREADY IMPLEMENTED
     (actually this should be WICK dominant for sweep candle, not body
      dominant -- see note below)
  4. The level being swept must be a significant level:
     - HTF FVG edge (15m+)
     - PDH/PDL
     - Session H/L
     - EQH/EQL (S or A tier)
     - NOT random 1m swing highs/lows alone

  IMPORTANT CORRECTION: For SWEEP candles specifically, the candle should
  be WICK-dominant (large wick showing rejection), not body-dominant.
  "Sweeps create large wicks" / "mostly wick = sweep"
  The body-dominance check should apply to the INVERSION candle, not
  the sweep candle. The sweep candle should show REJECTION (big wick).

  Post-sweep requirements:
  5. Fast reaction: displacement should follow within a few candles
  6. At least 1 body-dominant reversal candle within 20 bars -- IMPLEMENTED
  7. Sweep happens at/near an HTF key level (FVG, OB)
  8. No open liquidity behind the SL (nothing to re-sweep)

  Quality tiers for sweep:
  - A+: Sweeps accumulation (multiple touches), at HTF FVG, with SMT,
        after HTF candle open (PO3), immediate displacement
  - A: Sweeps significant level (PDH/PDL, session, EQH/EQL), at HTF FVG
  - B: Sweeps minor level, some displacement
  - FAIL: No clear level swept, slow reaction, body closes beyond level

STATUS: Mostly correct. The sweep candle body >= 50% filter may be
  WRONG for the sweep candle itself. Sweep candles should show
  REJECTION (wick-heavy), while the INVERSION candle should be
  body-dominant. Review sweep.py _check() body dominance logic.

F. RISK-REWARD MANAGEMENT
   Fixed 1R, or next major liquidity level?
========================================================================

RULING: TP at the NEXT MAJOR LIQUIDITY LEVEL (low-resistance liquidity),
not a fixed R multiple. BionicNQ focuses on "low hanging fruit" and
"engineered liquidity."

Source evidence:

1. BionicNQ (full_study.txt):
   "I can take the lowhanging fruit for this two RR trade and be
    completely fine."
   "I got my one to one and I was out of there."
   -- Takes whatever the first clean target gives, even if 1:1

2. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on targets:
   "Number one thing that I love to target is engineered highs. Always
    a great target. Low resistance liquidity runs happen on these."
   "Another one could be data highs."
   "Another one could be like an imbalance, like a high within a
    15 minute gap."
   "Focus on HTF Liquidity pools such as ITH/ITL, PDH/PDL, Data
    Highs/Data Lows, Session Liquidity"

3. BionicNQ ict_slides.txt (The Distribution):
   "Low Hanging Fruit" = "Obvious Liquidity pools, the Low resistance
    OF Low resistance"
   "High/Low that has to be taken in order to fulfill HTF DoL"
   "Targets align with HTF IRL<->ERL phase"
   "Unswept Liquidity - Unmitigated FVG(s)"
   "Previously Manipulated Liquidity -> Led to Distribution"

4. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on target selection:
   "We have three swings before our full draw. That's perfect liquidity."
   "You could have taken a TP here. This could be low hanging fruit."
   "Definitely not this high just because the RR would be horrible"
   -- Target selection considers both the level quality AND the RR

5. BionicNQ (full_study.txt) on IRL to ERL:
   "IRL to ERL emphasis. I'm entering on the IRL, targeting the ERL."
   "What are we doing? We already swept. Then what did we do? We tapped
    into IRL, internal range liquidity, which is that fair value gap.
    And where are we going? To external range liquidity."

6. Online (TrinityTrading):
   "Minimum acceptable R:R on an IFVG trade: 1:2"
   "First target: next area of liquidity in direction of your trade"

ACTIONABLE RULES FOR CODE:

  Target selection priority (in order):
  1. FIRST: Find the Draw on Liquidity (DOL) -- the HTF external range
     liquidity target (PDH/PDL, session extremes, data H/L, ITH/ITL)
  2. THEN: Check for "low-hanging fruit" = the FIRST significant
     opposing liquidity level between entry and DOL
  3. If low-hanging fruit exists and gives >= 1R, use it as TP1
  4. If low-hanging fruit gives < 1R, SKIP THE TRADE (don't force it)
  5. min_rr = 1.0 as absolute floor (BionicNQ took 1:1 multiple times)
  6. Ideal RR = 2:1+ when DOL is clear and path is unobstructed

  Target types ranked (best to worst):
  - Engineered highs/lows (multiple swing touches, ascending/descending)
  - Data highs/lows (news candle extremes)
  - PDH/PDL
  - Session H/L
  - EQH/EQL
  - HTF FVG CE (consequent encroachment)
  - Random swing H/L (worst, avoid if possible)

  Key principle: "LRL only" (Low Resistance Liquidity)
  - Target should be on a path with minimal opposing structure
  - Multiple swing points stacking toward target = A+ target
  - Isolated random high with no structural draw = bad target

STATUS: Current bot implementation (hard DOL requirement, LRL only,
  min_rr=1.0) is CORRECT and aligns with sources.

  [!] Sweep candle body dominance filter (sweep.py):

      Sources say sweep candles should be WICK-HEAVY (rejection).
      The body-dominance check makes sense for INVERSION candles
      but may be filtering out valid sweeps that show rejection wicks.
      RECOMMENDATION: Remove or invert body dominance check on sweep
      candle. A sweep candle with a big wick and small body is IDEAL.

  [!] BE logic (be_trigger_r=1.0):
      BionicNQ says BE should be conditional on reversal probability,
      not automatic at 1R. Current implementation is a reasonable
      mechanical approximation but may cause premature exits.
      RECOMMENDATION: Keep as-is for now; Priority 6 (BE at first
      internal H/L) would be more aligned with sources.

  [!] Inversion speed gate:
      BionicNQ says 3-4 candles max relative to the FVG timeframe.
      "5+ becomes iffy" (ict_slides.txt)
      "It should be one to two. Anything more than three and I'm like
       all right, what are we doing"
      RECOMMENDATION: Implement max_inversion_candles based on FVG TF:
      - 1m FVG: max 3-4 one-minute candles to invert
      - 5m FVG: max 3 five-minute candles (= 15 one-minute candles)
      This is listed as next priority in CLAUDE.md.

  [!] Sweep must happen at HTF key level:
      Sources heavily emphasize that the sweep must occur at a
      significant HTF level (15m+ FVG, PDH/PDL, etc.), not just
      any random swing. Current bot sweeps S/A/B tiers which
      partially covers this, but the HTF FVG co-location is not
      strictly required.
